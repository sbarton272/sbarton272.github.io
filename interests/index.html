Boy Scouts

"A dehydrated scout is a dead scout and dead scouts have no fun" I was involved in Boy Scouts for a number of years through my troop Rose Valley Troop 272, leadership courses and the Order of the Arrow. Backpacking, canoing, camping, climbing were all wonderful activities. I worked one summer at Philmont which was a most amazing summer experience.

FIRST Robotics

In high school I was on Team 486 the Positronic Panthers in the FIRST robotics competition. We had a grand time building a crude yet capable machine in our auto-shop and then going out on the game field and holding our own against the arc-welded machines of more privileged teams. I can thank robotics for getting me interested in engineering.

Fencing

In sixth grade I met Pixie Roane and was convined that fencing was the coolest sport ever invented. I joined the Wallingford Swarthmore Pather Fencing with Pixie Roane as my coach. I've continued fencing at school with the fencing club.

CMU Robotics Club

If you want to see the coolest stuff coming out of Carnegie Mellon undergrads, check out the RoboClub. I've been having fun here working on such projects as Tank Chair, Flutophone and Mechanical Logic Gates.

CMU Buggy: FRINGE

Carnegie Mellon University has a very strange, unique tradition called Buggy. The basic premise is soap-box derby on steroids. The driver is a tiny girl who is crammed into a super aerodynamic bullet-shaped carbon fiber vehicle and then sent careening down a hill at 40mph. It was good fun and constructing the vehicles is quite the challenge. If this intrigues you check out the website or skip that and just look at crash videos .

Sailing

My father's family grew up with wet feet. We joke that they learned to sail before learning to walk. While watered-down slightly, that tradition was kept alive. Sailing is an interesting activity because its as much of a mental game than anything else. While the young and strong certainly have an advantage, sailing bridges the generations. I know I'll be drawn to water for the rest of my life because its in my blood.

The News

I've always been a bit of a news junkie. I really enjoy the Christian Science Monitor as a fairly unbiased, outside the mainstream news sources. Of course the New York Times continues to be well written. Recently, I've taken an interest in a more outside perspective on US news and have turned to the BBC and Al Jazera as two good sources. Maybe I'm a snob, but I've personally found the written word to be more informative than television news.
